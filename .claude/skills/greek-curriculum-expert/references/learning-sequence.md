# Learning Sequence for Production-Focused Greek

This is a phase-by-phase breakdown of what to introduce and when. Each phase assumes the previous phase's content is being actively drilled (not just "covered").

The phases are not rigid levels. They're waves: you start Phase 2 content while still drilling Phase 1 material. The goal is overlapping reinforcement, not sequential completion.

## Phase 1: Survival Production

**Goal**: Produce basic present-tense sentences about self, wants, and immediate environment.

**Duration**: First 2-3 months of active drilling.

### Verbs (present tense only)
Drill these 15 verbs to automaticity before adding more:

| Priority | Verb | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| 1 | είμαι | Identity, description, location |
| 2 | έχω | Possession, age, "there is" |
| 3 | θέλω | Wants, requests |
| 4 | μπορώ | Ability, permission |
| 5 | κάνω | General action, weather, "how are you" |
| 6 | πηγαίνω/πάω | Movement, plans |
| 7 | μιλάω | Communication |
| 8 | τρώω | Daily routine |
| 9 | πίνω | Daily routine |
| 10 | δουλεύω | Work/routine |
| 11 | μένω | Living, staying |
| 12 | βλέπω | Seeing, watching |
| 13 | ακούω | Hearing, understanding |
| 14 | ξέρω | Knowing |
| 15 | αγαπάω | Liking, loving |

Conjugate only sg1, sg2, sg3 initially. Add plurals once singulars are automatic.

### Nouns
Start with 30-40 high-frequency nouns. Prioritise:
- Nouns the learner uses in daily life (food, home, family, work)
- Nouns that appear in common phrases
- A mix of genders to build article intuition

**Critical**: Drill nouns with their articles. "σπίτι" is useless; "το σπίτι" is Greek.

### Cases
- **Nominative**: subject position. Comes free with learning nouns.
- **Accusative**: direct objects and after basic prepositions (σε, με, για). This is the production priority. Most sentences need accusative.

Skip genitive and vocative entirely in Phase 1.

### Chunks and phrases
Treat these as atomic units, not grammar:
- Με λένε... (My name is...)
- Θα ήθελα... (I would like...)
- Πού είναι...; (Where is...?)
- Πόσο κάνει...; (How much is...?)
- Μπορώ να...; (Can I...?)
- Πρέπει να... (I need to...)

### Word order
Greek is flexible but defaults to SVO. Don't teach word order as a topic. Let it emerge from drilling sentences.

### Prerequisites for Phase 2
Before moving to Phase 2, the learner should be able to:
- Produce present tense of the 15 core verbs in sg1/sg2/sg3 within 5 seconds
- Produce accusative of 20+ common nouns with correct article within 5 seconds
- Produce 10+ chunks from English prompt within 3 seconds

## Phase 2: Past Narrative

**Goal**: Talk about what happened. Tell stories about yesterday, last week, the trip.

**Duration**: Months 3-6 of active drilling.

### Verbs (aorist)
Same 15 verbs, now in aorist. This is where Greek gets hard because aorist stems are irregular. Group by stem pattern, not alphabetically:

| Pattern | Verbs | Aorist sg1 |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Regular -σα | δουλεύω, μιλάω, ακούω | δούλεψα, μίλησα, άκουσα |
| Suppletive | τρώω, πίνω, βλέπω | έφαγα, ήπια, είδα |
| -κα | κάνω | έκανα |
| -γα | πηγαίνω | πήγα |

Drill the suppletive forms hardest. They can't be guessed; they must be memorised.

Add 5-10 more verbs at this phase:
- έρχομαι (ήρθα) - coming
- παίρνω (πήρα) - taking/getting
- λέω (είπα) - saying
- φεύγω (έφυγα) - leaving
- βρίσκω (βρήκα) - finding
- δίνω (έδωσα) - giving
- γράφω (έγραψα) - writing
- διαβάζω (διάβασα) - reading
- μαθαίνω (έμαθα) - learning
- αγοράζω (αγόρασα) - buying

### Cases
- **Genitive**: introduce for possession only (του Νίκου, της Μαρίας, μου/σου/του). Don't drill full genitive paradigms yet.
- **Accusative**: expand to more nouns. Drill article changes (τον/την/το).

### Weak object pronouns
Critical production skill. Start with accusative clitics:
- με, σε, τον, την, το, μας, σας, τους, τις, τα

Drill placement: before the verb in most cases (Τον βλέπω, Την αγαπάω).

### Time expressions
Teach as chunks:
- χτες (yesterday), σήμερα (today), αύριο (tomorrow)
- πέρσι (last year), φέτος (this year)
- την περασμένη εβδομάδα (last week)
- τον περασμένο μήνα (last month)

### Prerequisites for Phase 3
- Produce aorist of 20+ verbs within 5 seconds
- Use weak object pronouns in correct position
- Tell a simple past narrative (3-4 sentences) from English prompt

## Phase 3: Expanding Range

**Goal**: Talk about future plans, give commands, describe things with adjectives.

**Duration**: Months 6-10 of active drilling.

### Verbs (future + imperative)
- **Future**: θα + subjunctive stem. The key insight: the subjunctive stem is often the same as the aorist stem without the augment. θα φάω, θα πιω, θα δω. Drill as "θα + [aorist-like form]" not as a separate paradigm.
- **Imperative**: perfective imperative of 10-15 common verbs. Κάνε!, Έλα!, Πες!, Φέρε!, Δώσε! These are high-frequency in real speech.

### Adjective agreement
Introduce adjective agreement for nominative and accusative only. The learner needs:
- Masculine/feminine/neuter nominative (ωραίος/ωραία/ωραίο)
- Masculine/feminine/neuter accusative (ωραίο/ωραία/ωραίο)

Start with the 10 most common adjectives:
καλός, κακός, μεγάλος, μικρός, ωραίος, νέος, παλιός, ζεστός, κρύος, πολύς

Don't drill genitive or vocative adjective forms. Don't drill full paradigm tables.

### Genitive expansion
- After prepositions: κοντά σε → κοντά στο/στη/στον
- With more nouns: expand beyond just names to common nouns in genitive

### Noun plurals
Introduce plurals for high-frequency nouns. Group by pattern:
- -ος → -οι (δρόμος → δρόμοι)
- -α → -ες (θάλασσα → θάλασσες)
- -ο → -α (βιβλίο → βιβλία)
- -ι → -ια (παιδί → παιδιά)

### Conditional
Θα + imperfect for unreal conditions. Teach as a chunk pattern:
- Θα ήθελα... (I would like)
- Θα μπορούσα... (I could)
- Θα έπρεπε... (I should)

## Phase 4: Towards Fluency

**Goal**: Handle complex expression, nuance, and less common structures.

**Duration**: Months 10+ of active drilling.

### Passive voice
Only for high-frequency verbs where passive is common in speech:
- κοιμάμαι (I sleep, technically medio-passive)
- φοβάμαι (I'm afraid)
- θυμάμαι (I remember)
- χρειάζομαι (I need)
- σκέφτομαι (I think)

Don't drill passive as a "voice transformation" exercise. These are just verbs that happen to be passive in form.

### Full pronoun system
- Genitive clitics: μου, σου, του/της, μας, σας, τους
- Double pronoun placement: Μου το δίνει (He gives it to me)
- Strong vs weak forms

### Relative clauses
- που (invariable relative pronoun, covers 90% of cases)
- Skip ο οποίος/η οποία/το οποίο until advanced

### Genitive with prepositions
Full genitive usage beyond possession. This is genuinely hard and low-frequency in casual speech. Defer as long as possible.

### Subjunctive uses
- Να + verb for wishes, purposes, suggestions
- Distinguish from θα + verb (future) at production speed

---

## What NOT to sequence at all

These exist in Greek grammar but don't belong in a production-focused app for intermediate learners:

- Full paradigm tables (teach forms, not tables)
- Katharevousa / archaic forms
- Participles (except frozen forms)
- Formal/scholarly genitive constructions
- Dual number (doesn't exist in Modern Greek, but some textbooks mention it)
- Detailed phonological rules (teach pronunciation by example)
- Grammatical metalanguage ("dative" doesn't exist in MG, but some textbooks ghost it)
